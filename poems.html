poems

a quiet collection

morning cat

she stretches across the windowsill as if the sunlight owes her something— and maybe it does. she has never learned to ask for less than everything.

small hours

at 3am the house breathes differently, slower, softer, like it finally has room to exhale. i sit with a cup of something warm and watch the dark and for once ask nothing of it.

after rain

the petrichor is just a word for something we already know— that the earth, too, has been waiting.

routine

she comes to the bowl at the same time every morning, certain only of this: that it will be full, that i will be there, that some things do not change. i try to be as dependable as her hunger.

distance

the stars are old light, arriving late, patient as grief— still bright enough to navigate by.
